# geo411-Regression
R analysis of precipitation data for Buffalo and San Diego My methods of analyzing the data was using RStudio to compute different percentages allowing me to explore variability, uncertainty, and change over time. I also used outputs from RStudio to perform two-sample difference of means tests to determine whether the mean annual precipitation is significantly different during the two time periods (1940-1982 versus 1983-2025). In the first t-test the null hypothesis was rejected so the later period had precipitation in the later peiod. In the second t-test the null hypothesis was rejected therefore amount of precipitation between 1940-1982 has less amount of percipitation than the later period. In the third t-test the null hypothesis was rejected therefore amount of precipitation was greater in the later period than the earlier period. The fourth t-test the null hypothesis was accepted and shows that San Diego's early period had slightly more precipitation than late. I used RStudio and computed the answers using statisical methods provided. The different t-tests show the precipitation differenced between Buffalo and San Diego. In Buffalo, the later period 1983-2025 has a higher average precipitation than the earlier period of 1940-1982. This shows that precipitation has increased over time in Buffalo. San Diego showed slight difference between the early and late periods. The change was not statistically significant as it was only −0.0516. This shows that Buffalo is experiencing an increasing rate of precipitation over time while, San Diego’s precipitation has remained relatively stable with slight decrease in precipitation.
